Patricia E. Jiang
Patricia is a first-year student in BCIT's Digital Development and Design program. After working in psychology research for seven years, Patricia decided to pursue a career change to allow more creativity and happiness into her life. She is the owner of two cats, Sherlock and Watson, and tries hard to make friends with every cat she comes across in daily life.

Here are some of Patricia's favourite creative projects over the years!

Coffee, anybody?

This realistic vector drawing of an espresso machine was created by Patricia as a part of her studies at BCIT.

As an avid fan of singer Taylor Swift, Patricia created a vector logo inspired by Swift's album, Lover.
In an ideal world, Patricia would be surrounded by cats and plants while sleeping in a room with floor-to-ceiling windows.
Patricia graduated in 2017 from UBC's Theatre Production and Design program. During this program, she sewed a full Victorian-era costume, complete with lining and a bustle.
In Patricia's opinion, Halloween is the best holiday of the year! This magazine cover was created with this holiday in mind using Photoshop software.
At home, Patricia loves to tinker around with a sewing machine. She has created fabric-based products such as utensil holders, aprons, and reusable facial cotton squares.
The most defining sewing project of her life was this custom tulle and lace overskirt, created for her older sister's wedding.
Patricia's main role in the theatre industry was in stage management. "And Bella Sang With Us" was Patricia's first professional show which ended up winning the title of "Pick of the Fringe 2016"!
With theatre, each production is completely different. Patricia is proud to have worked on hard-hitting shows such as "C'Mon Angie!" with Touchstone Theatre, "Betrayal" with Ensemble Theatre Company, and Les Belles Soeurs with UBC Theatre.
